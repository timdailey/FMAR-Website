## Not All Directories Carry the Same Weight

Every local business guide recommends the same starting list: Google Business Profile, Yelp, the Better Business Bureau, maybe Angi. That list is a reasonable default for a home services company or a restaurant. It's incomplete for a plastic surgery practice, because AI platforms draw on category-specific sources when they're deciding which practice to recommend for a medical or cosmetic procedure, and three platforms in particular carry outsized weight in this category: RealSelf, Zwivel, and Vitals.

## RealSelf: The Procedure-Level Trust Signal

RealSelf is built specifically around cosmetic procedures. Patients leave detailed reviews tied to a specific procedure and a specific practice, and rate whether the procedure was "Worth It," a rating mechanism unique to the platform. This procedure-level structure is exactly the kind of data AI platforms can use to answer a specific question like "is a mommy makeover worth it" or "best rhinoplasty surgeon in Denver" with a named recommendation, rather than a generic answer.

A thin or outdated RealSelf profile is one of the most common gaps we see in practice audits. Claiming the profile is only the first step. Actively encouraging patients to leave detailed, procedure-specific reviews, and responding to them, is what turns the listing into a real signal.

## Vitals: The Credential and Board-Certification Layer

Vitals surfaces physician credential data, including board certification, more prominently than most general review platforms. For a category where credentials are a core part of the trust decision, that makes Vitals a meaningfully different kind of signal than a star rating alone. AI platforms evaluating whether to recommend a provider for a medical procedure have reason to weight verified credential data heavily, and Vitals is one of the more accessible, well-structured sources of that data.

Keeping a Vitals profile current, correcting any outdated credential information, and making sure it matches what's stated on your own website closes a gap that's easy to overlook once a profile has been claimed and forgotten.

## Zwivel: The Newer Platform With Growing Influence

Zwivel is smaller than RealSelf or Vitals, but it's grown quickly as a consultation and booking platform specifically for cosmetic procedures. Its structure, procedure listings, pricing ranges, and direct consultation requests, gives it a different kind of signal: active booking behavior rather than only historical reviews. As AI platforms increasingly reference platforms with structured, current activity data, Zwivel's role in this space is worth monitoring even though it doesn't yet carry the volume of RealSelf.

## Where Generic Directories Still Fit

None of this means Yelp, the Better Business Bureau, or Google Business Profile should be ignored. Google Business Profile in particular remains foundational for general local search visibility. But when the specific goal is being recommended by name for a cosmetic procedure question, RealSelf, Zwivel, and Vitals deserve the larger share of a practice's reputation management effort, not the generic local directory list built for a plumber or a restaurant.
