## Why a Checklist, Not a Guess

Most plastic surgery practices have never formally checked whether AI platforms recommend them. This checklist covers the four signal categories that actually determine AI visibility for a cosmetic surgery practice, so you can audit your own practice in under an hour before deciding what, if anything, needs professional help.

## Category One: Google Business Profile

- Profile is claimed and verified, not just auto-generated.
- Primary category is set correctly (Plastic Surgeon, not a generic Doctor or Medical Clinic category).
- Additional categories cover your specific procedure specialties where Google allows it.
- Business name, address, and phone number match exactly across your website and every other listing.
- At least 20 to 30 recent reviews, with a steady monthly pace rather than a burst followed by silence.
- Photos are current and include the practice, not just stock imagery.

## Category Two: Reputation Platforms Specific to Medical Aesthetics

- RealSelf profile is claimed, complete, and actively monitored for new reviews.
- Zwivel listing exists and reflects current procedures offered.
- Vitals profile includes complete, accurate board-certification and credential information.
- Any discrepancies between your name, address, or phone number on these platforms and your primary listings are corrected.
- Response rate to reviews (positive and negative) on all three platforms is reasonably current, not months behind.

## Category Three: Structured Data on Your Website

- Physician or MedicalOrganization schema is present and includes correct specialty and credential fields.
- Individual Service schema exists for your core procedures, not just a single generic service listing.
- FAQPage schema is implemented on pages that already contain question-and-answer content.
- Review or AggregateRating schema reflects your actual review data, once you have enough reviews to display meaningfully.
- GeoCoordinates and areaServed fields are present if you serve a defined region or multiple cities.

## Category Four: Procedure-Level Content and Visibility

- Dedicated pages exist for each major procedure you perform, not a single combined services page.
- Each procedure page directly answers common patient questions in the first paragraph, not buried deep in the content.
- Before-and-after content includes descriptive alt text and, where possible, structured captions.
- You've tested AI platforms directly with procedure-specific queries ("best [procedure] in [your city]") to confirm whether you're cited, not just brand-level queries.
- Comparison or "what to expect" content exists for procedures where patients commonly compare multiple options before deciding.
