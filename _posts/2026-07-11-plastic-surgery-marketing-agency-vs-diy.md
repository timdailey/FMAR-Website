## A Decision That Used to Have Two Options Now Has a Third Variable

Deciding whether to hire a plastic surgery marketing agency or keep marketing in-house used to come down to bandwidth and budget. Does your team have the time and the specific skills to run SEO, manage reputation platforms, and execute paid campaigns well? If not, an agency fills the gap.

That calculus hasn't gone away, but it now includes a channel most practices, and most agencies, are still figuring out: AI visibility. Whether ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google's AI Overview actually name your practice when a prospective patient asks for a recommendation is no longer optional to check. It's become part of the same conversation as SEO and reputation management, and it changes what "good marketing" needs to cover.

## What DIY Marketing Can Still Handle Well

A capable in-house marketing coordinator or manager can absolutely run reputation management, basic SEO, and social content without an agency. Practices that do this well usually share a few traits: someone on the team owns Google Business Profile and review generation as an explicit responsibility, the website gets regular content updates rather than sitting static for years, and there's at least a working knowledge of what schema markup is and why it matters.

AI visibility work fits naturally into this list for teams that already have this foundation. The core tasks, checking your RealSelf and Vitals profile completeness, verifying your Google Business Profile is fully built out, and making sure your website's structured data correctly describes your practice and procedures, aren't fundamentally different skills than good SEO practice. They're an extension of it.

## Where DIY Marketing Usually Falls Short

The gap shows up in three places most often. First, paid media at scale, campaigns across multiple platforms with meaningful budget usually need dedicated expertise to avoid wasted spend, especially given the ad review restrictions common in cosmetic and medical categories. Second, multi-location coordination, once a practice has two or more offices, keeping reputation signals, local SEO, and AI visibility consistent across every location becomes a much bigger job. Third, and most relevant here, most in-house teams have never specifically tested their AI visibility, because until recently there wasn't an easy way to do it, and nobody was asking the question.

## What to Look for in a Plastic Surgery Marketing Agency

If you're evaluating agencies, the AI visibility conversation is a useful filter. Ask directly whether they test and report on AI mention rates across ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Claude, not just Google rankings and ad performance. Ask whether their reputation management work extends to RealSelf, Zwivel, and Vitals specifically, since those platforms carry more weight in this category than the generic local directories a general-purpose agency might default to. And ask for a specific example of a client's AI visibility before and after their engagement, not just a traffic or ranking report.

An agency that can't answer these questions in specific terms is likely running the same playbook they'd run for any local business category, without the medical aesthetics-specific tuning this space actually needs.
