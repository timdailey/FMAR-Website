Common questions

  • AI visibility is how often — and how accurately — your business appears in answers generated by AI platforms when buyers ask questions about your industry. As AI becomes the default starting point for product research, companies that don't appear in those answers are being removed from consideration before a single sales conversation happens. AI visibility is now a direct driver of pipeline, not just a brand metric.

  • Traditional search rankings and AI citations are driven by different signals. Google rankings depend on keyword relevance, backlinks, and page authority. AI systems weight entity clarity, Wikipedia and Wikidata presence, schema markup, third-party validation, and content structured around direct questions. A company can rank number one on Google and still be invisible to AI platforms — and most are.

  • The report maps your current position across the major AI platforms, identifies which buyer-intent queries you appear in and which you don't, and benchmarks your entity signals, content gaps, and schema markup against your top competitors. Every report closes with a prioritized action plan organized by impact and implementation effort — specific directional actions to help you prioritize where to start — not a replacement for a full technical audit.

  • Several factors that influence AI citation can show results within weeks, not months. Schema markup improvements are often reflected in AI answers within two to four weeks. New FAQ pages and structured content typically get indexed and cited within four to eight weeks. Entity profile improvements — Wikipedia, Wikidata, and review platform presence — take longer but have the most durable long-term impact. Results in your free snapshot are based on a point-in-time analysis and should be treated as a baseline, not a final audit.

  • The report covers the major AI answer platforms where B2B buyers are conducting research today, including conversational AI assistants and AI-powered search overviews.
