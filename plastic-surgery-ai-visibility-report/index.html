Why this matters

Patients Are Asking AI Before They Ask Google

A growing share of "who's the best plastic surgeon near me" research now starts in ChatGPT, Perplexity, or a Google AI Overview, not a search results page.

What is AI visibility for a practice?

How often, and how accurately, your practice is named when someone asks an AI platform a question like "best rhinoplasty surgeon in Austin" or "top-rated breast augmentation near me." It's the map pack, reborn as a conversational recommendation.

Why your Google ranking isn't enough

A strong Google presence doesn't guarantee an AI recommendation. AI platforms weigh RealSelf and Zwivel review volume, board-certification signals, and independent third-party confirmation, not just your own website.

What's at stake

A prospective patient can search for exactly the procedure you specialize in, in your city, and never hear your name if your AI signals aren't in place. That's a consult lost before your team ever picks up the phone.

The good news: it's fixable

AI visibility for a practice comes down to a short, well-defined list of signals: your review platforms, your schema, and your listings. No rebrand required, just knowing exactly which ones are missing.

Built for this category

Why Plastic Surgery Marketing Needs a Different Playbook

Generic local SEO tools check Yelp, Angi, and the Better Business Bureau. Those platforms barely register when an AI model is deciding which plastic surgeon to recommend. This report checks the sources that actually carry weight in medical aesthetics: RealSelf, where patients read verified reviews and "Worth It" ratings; Zwivel, a growing consultation and booking platform AI models increasingly cite; and Vitals, which carries board-certification and credential data AI systems use to validate a surgeon's legitimacy.

We also test procedure-specific queries, not just brand-level searches. A practice can be well-cited for "plastic surgeon in Miami" and completely absent for "mommy makeover Miami" or "revision rhinoplasty Miami," and those procedure-level gaps are where consults are actually being lost.
